Abstract

Image/video compression has been a remarkable research for both academia and industry in recent years. Testing datasets, especially high quality image/video datasets are desirable for efficient validation of related compression researches, practical applications and standardization activities. In this paper, we put forward a test dataset for compressionrelated systems, named USTC-TD. The proposed dataset has been Successfully applied in the practical end-to-end image/video compression challenge of IEEE International Conference on Visual Communications and lmage Processing (VCIP Challenge) in 2022 and 2023. USTC-TD contains 40 images at 2160p (4K) spatial resolution and 10 sequences at 1080P spatial resolution, which is captured in diverse content covering different environment conditions and scene types. Furthermore, we evaluate the proposed dataset with different metrics (PSNR, MS-SSIM, et al.) on recent advanced traditional and learned compression algorithms, such as learned image compression schemes (Factorized Model, Hyperprior Model, Autoregressive Model , Cheng2020, iWave++, ELIC, MLIC+) , learned video compression schemes (DVC Pro, CANF-VC, DCVC, TCM-VC, HEM, OOFE, SDD, VNVC, DCVC-DC, DCVC-FM) , standardization activities (BPG, High Efficiency Video Coding (HEVC), Versatile Video Coding (VVC), AOMedia Video 1 and 2 (AV1 and AV2) , IEEE 1857.11 , AVS-EEM), and et al. We provide an extended baseline for state-of-the-art compression systems benchmarked on USTC-TD.

Analysis

To further verify the wide coverage and diversity of our proposed dataset for different scenes, we evaluate the USTCTD on different image/video features, and compare it with the previous image/video common test datasets. For analysis of image/video features, we select spatial information (SI), temporal information (TI), colourfulness (CF) and lightness information (LI) to characterize each dataset along the dimensions of color, space, and temporal correlation, which is commonly used to evaluate the quality of dataset.
